  • * no writer, equally with M. de Buffon proves the power of eloquence, & uncertainty of theories. he takes any hypothesis whatever or it's reverse, & furnishes explanations equally specious and persuasive. thus in his XVIII vol. wishing to explain why the largest animals are found in the torrid zone, he assumes heat as the efficient principle of the animal volume. speaking of America he says, 'la terre y est froide, impuissante a produire les principes actifs, a developer les germes des plus grands quadrupedes, auxquels il faut, pour croitre et se multiplier, toute la chaleur, toute l' activité que le soleil peut donner a la terre, amoureuse.' pa. 156. 'l'ardeur des hommes et la grandeur des animaux dependent de la salubrité, et de la chaleur de l'air.' ib. 160. in his Epochs again when it is become convenient to his theory to consider the bones of the mammoth found in the coldest regions, as the bones of the elephant, & necessary to explain how the elephant there should have been six times as large as that of the torrid zone, it is cold which produces animal volume. 'tout ce qu'il y a de colossal et de grand dans la nature, a eté formé dans les terres du nord.' 1. Epoq. 255. 'C'est dans les regions de notre Nord que la nature vivante s'est elevée a ses plus grandes dimensions.' ib. 263.
An asterisk in text appears after "bewitching language." The corresponding note begins in the right margin and fills much of the bottom margin. In the 1853 edition, this material is incorporated in a footnote. Translations by Maximilian Schele de Vere are as follows: "The earth is cold, unable to produce the principles necessary for the development of the germs of the largest quadrupeds, which require, for their growth and propagation, all the heat and activity which the sun can give to the loving earth" and "The temper of man and the size of animals depend upon the salubrity and the heat of the air" and "All that is colossal and grand in Nature has been formed in Northern countries" and finally, "It is in our Northern regions that living nature has risen to the largest dimensions."
