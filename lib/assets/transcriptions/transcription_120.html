A plus sign in pencil appears above Jefferson's margin note "1 Clavigero. 120." The corresponding plus mark appears in the text after "introduced among them." At the end of Logan's speech a ⊕ mark appears in pencil. The corresponding mark at the bottom of the page refers to the letter of J.B. Gibson in the fourth Appendix printed in the 1853 edition. The Gibson letter belongs to a controversy over the authenticity of Logan's speech. After a letter challenging the accuracy of the Logan's narrative by a son-in-law of Cresap named Luther Martin was published in a Baltimore paper on March 29, 1797, Jefferson began collecting evidence to certify that the speech was not a forgery. Jefferson published a number of these letters and depositions in 1800 in a quarto volume titled An Appendix to the Notes on Virginia Relative to the Murder of Logan's Family (Philadephia: Samuel H. Smith, 1800). Much of this documentary evidence was reprinted in a lengthy appendix to the 1853 edition of the Notes, "Relative to the Murder of Logan's Family," which includes a letter of response from Jefferson, the certificates and depositions, and a historical statement on the affair.
