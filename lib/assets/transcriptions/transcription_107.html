  • Perros en la Española han crecido en numero y en grandeza, de suerte que es plaga de aquella isla. Acosta IV. 33.
A plus sign appears, in pencil, after "favour of America" and above the printed margin note "XVIII. 96." A small circle mark in pencil appears above the printed margin note "IX. 41." and after "of the middle size" in text. Pencilled plus signs inside circles (⊕) appear above Jefferson's inked marginal citation and in the text: the inked citation is connected, by corresponding pencil mark, to "individual is possessed." Jefferson cites José de Acosta's Historia natural y moral de las indias. The annotation is not faithfully transcribed in the footnote that appears in the 1853 edition (see page 61). Maximilian Schele de Vere translates the Spanish as follows: "Dogs have in Hispaniola grown so much in number and in size as to become the plague of that island."
