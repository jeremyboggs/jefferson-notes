A difficult page to transcribe. Something has been lightly pencilled in the left margin and rubbed away: "one [in?] W..s.[m?].g ..l en... of.. l.[k?]. Fl[e?]mi[ng?]. Be au... .i.t." The two ※ marks (known as "asteriskos"), are in pencil and traditionally signal to a printer to switch one line with another. The reference to Samuel Pleasants Junior's Virginia Argus is written between the paragraphs and disappears into the binding, making it impossible to transcribe the manuscript addition beyond the first two numerals "10" and the "t" ("broke out 10[0 feet] higher up t[he] hill."). We follow the printing of the 1853 edition and interpolate "0 feet" and "he".
