A plus sign in pencil appears after "defective in ardor," and in the margin above Jefferson's annotation, in ink, "Amer. Vesp. 13. 'fuora di misura lussuriosi &c. 108." A small circle mark, in pencil, appears in the margin above the second citation, in ink, added by Jefferson. The corresponding mark appears in the text after "on bravery;". The two quotations from Amerigo Vespucci appear at the bottom of the page as untranslated footnotes in the 1853 edition and are translated in Professor Schele de Vere's appendix.). They may be translated, respectively, as "sensual beyond measure" and "of good strength and great spirit." Jefferson here collects evidence to defend the native Americans from climactic accusations of New World degeneration.
