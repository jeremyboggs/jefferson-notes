The first plus mark, in ink, appears in the text after "honey-bee in Brazil." The second, in pencil, appears above Jefferson's first margin note. Two small circle marks in pencil match the next margin note to the text "white settlers." Two ⊕ marks appear after "winter in Canada." and above margin note "I. 126." The first note is translated by Schele de Vere as follows: "See Herrera, Dec. 1, L. 10, chap. 8. 'When Yucatan was discovered, an abundance of wax and honey was found."—And ib. ch. 9. 'There are found hornets and bees, although the latter are smaller, and sting with more fury.'—Dec. 2, L. 3, ch. 1." The second annotation is translated, "See Clavigero, 107. On the frontier of Guayaquil there are found bees, which accumulate and make honey in the hollows of trees; they are larger than flies; the wax and the honey they make are red, and although it tastes well, it is not the same as in Castille." Herr. 5, 10, 10."
