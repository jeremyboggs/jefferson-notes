A plus sign in pencil appears above Jefferson's annotation. The corresponding plus appears in the text of the footnote after "parts of the earth." The 1853 edition of Jefferson's Notes incorporates this manuscript addition in the footnote. The Italian has been translated by Schele de Vere as follows: "Here the sky and the air are seldom darkened by clouds; the days are almost always clear." The 1765 edition of Vespucci, which elsewhere corresponds to Jefferson's citations, shows this quotation appearing on page 113.
