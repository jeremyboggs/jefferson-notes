  • 'la terre est est demeurée froide, impuissante a produire les principes actifs, a developer les germes des plus grands quadrupedes, auxquels il faut, pour croitre et se multiplier, toute la chaleur, toute l' activité que le soleil peut donner a la terre, amoureuse.' xviii. 156. 'L'ardeur des hommes et la grandeur des animaux dependent de la salubrité de la chaleur de l’air. ib. 160.
Three plus signs in pencil coordinate a printed margin note, an in-text reference to Buffon's opinion, and the annotation written in ink in the bottom margin. Maximilian Schele de Vere translates the French as follows: "'The earth has (since) remained cold, unable to produce the principles necessary for the development of the germs of the largest quadrupeds, which require for their growth and propagation all the heat and activity which the sun can give to the loving earth.' — Xviii. 156. 'The temper of men and the size of animals depend upon the salubrity and the heat of the air.' — Ib. 160."
