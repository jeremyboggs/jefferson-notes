A plus, in ink, appears after "others," in line 10. In the bottom margin the quotation from Tertullian's Ad Scapulam appears. The passage has been described as one of the earliest expressions of the principle of freedom of religion: "However, it is a natural and human right that each should worship as he believes. A man's religion neither harms nor profits another. It is even contrary to religion to coerce religion, which ought to be embraced voluntarily and not by violence."
