In the printed text an asterisk appears before "Instances." The corresponding note appears in the left margin. The end of this clause, "both hemispheres;" is marked with a superscripted circle [º]. In the gutter the corresponding note appears but is neither legible in the scans nor when the physical artifact is inspected. Where Jefferson's manuscript addition disappears into the binding, we draw from the 1853 edition. (Compare "º 2 Epoques, [276,] in Mexico; b[ut] 1. Epoques, 25[0,] denies the fa[ct] as to S. Two pluses or crosses add more annotations in the left margin: the first corresponding to "last mentioned." and the second to "river-horse." Maximilian Schele de Vere's translation of the first annotation is as follows: "Clavigero says: 'I do not remember that any American nation has any tradition of elephants, or hippopotami, or other quadrupeds of equal size. I do not know that any of the numerous excavations made in New Spain has brought to light the carcass of a hippopotamus, or even the tooth of an elephant.'—125." The second annotation is translated by Maximilian Schele de Vere as follows: "2. Epoques, 232. Buffon pronounces it is not the grinder either of the elephant or hippopotamus, but of a species, 'the first and the greatest of all land animals now lost.'"
