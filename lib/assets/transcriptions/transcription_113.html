  • 'vivono cento cinquanta anni.' Amer. Vesp. III.
Jefferson's citation appears at the bottom of the page, in ink. The 1853 edition includes it with the preceding footnote; although it appears to stand alone here. The full sentence in the Italian original—"Vivono cento cinquanta anni, per quanto si puote intendere, e rare volte s' infermano, e se per forte cadono in qualche infermit√†, subitamente si medicano con sugo d' erbe"—asserts of the natives that they live one hundred and fifty years, are rarely sick, and that when some infirmity befalls them, they medicate themselves with an herb sauce. The number "65" and then a pencilled ratio ("65/5"), the fourth ratio to appear in the book, appears in the right margin. Two colons, mark the reset pagination in the 1853 edition: the first separates "that" and "his" in the main text, the second separates "and" and "told" in the footnoted text. At the bottom of page 65, in the new edition, the signature "5" is printed.
