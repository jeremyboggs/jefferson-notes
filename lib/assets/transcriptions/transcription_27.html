  • it is said however that, at a very moderate expence the whole current of the upper part of the Kanhaway may be turned into the South fork of the Roanoke, the Allegany there subsiding, and the two rivers approaching so near, that a canal of 9. miles long. and of 30. feet depth, at the deepest part, would draw the water of the Kanhaway into this branch of the Roanoke. this canal would be in Montgomery county, the courthouse of which is on the top of the Allegany.
All additions are in ink and in Jefferson's hand. These revisions are made to the paragraph on The Great Kanhaway. The first phrase is inserted by means of a caret that splits the two words "James river" and adds an "s", which pluralizes the printed word "river" and writes over the period that had punctuated the sentence. Throughout his personal copy, as here, Jefferson mixes his typographic handwriting with the printed page. The longer annotation begins at the end of the paragraph and then reappears in the left margin, finally spilling into the bottom margin.
