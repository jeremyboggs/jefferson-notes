Plus signs in pencil appear above both of Jefferson's marginal annotations, written in ink. A plus sign corresponding to the first marginal annotation appears in the text after "time after." The missing fourth plus sign appears on the next page. Immediately below the second annotation, in pencil, are the instructions to see the next page. The fragment from Vespucci appears in a footnote to the 1853 edition. Jefferson quotes from the account of the first voyage. The sentence in full — "Sono donne molto generative, e nelle loro pregnezze non scusano travaglio alcuno; i loro parti son tanto leggieri, che partorito d' un di, vanno fuora per tutto, e massime a lavarsi a' fiumi, e stanno sane come pesci" — explains that because their birth-pains are light, native women who go out to the river to wash themselves after labor remain "as healthy as a fish."
