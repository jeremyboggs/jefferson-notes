The first manuscript addition appears in the list of species, following the printed "Query species?" (A similar in-text query appears on the previous page.) The Notes is a restless, searching text in which answers to queries—namely Jefferson's answers to Marbois' initial queries—are productive of new queries. Here Jefferson's annotated answer, "fluvialis" (provided in ink), is taken from William Bartram's Travels, published in Philadelphia in 1791. Jefferson gives "fluvialis" for the species name "fluviatilis" and the same is printed on page 393 in the 1791 edition of Bartram's Travels. More recently, this separate name for the so-called "river willow" has been rejected: see the English version of the International Code of Botanical Nomenclature Adopted by the Seventeenth International Botanical Congress, ed. J. McNeill et al. (2006), accessed October 15, 2011, http://ibot.sav.sk/icbn/main.htm.
